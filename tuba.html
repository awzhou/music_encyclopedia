The Tuba

The tuba is a member of the brass family. Out of all the brass instruments, it is the lowest pitched. The tuba is most commonly used to play the bass line in pieces due to its bass voice. Although it seems like an unlikely solo instrument, an increasing number of solo works are being written for the tuba. In the orchestra, the tuba sits in the back with the rest of the bass voices.
